Tharra

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
Jump to navigation Jump to search

Tharra (Hindi: ठर्रा ) is a type of Desi daru which is locally, and often illegally, brewed alcoholic drink in North India and Pakistan . [1] It is made from yeast fermentation of sugarcane, or wheat husk, especially in Bihar , Uttar Pradesh , Punjab and Haryana . Since it is usually made from sugarcane, it is often viewed as a crude rum . [2]

Preparation [ edit ]

Due to the pungent smell of the distilling process, Tharra is often prepared in remote fields, away from human settlements. It recycles some of the waste products of agricultural economy of the region.

Local economy [ edit ]

It is often consumed by poor or landless tillers, typically distilled with connivance of officials and police officers, generating large profits.
