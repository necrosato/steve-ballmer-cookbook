The Chicago Cocktail is a brandy -based mixed drink probably named for the city of Chicago, Illinois . [1] It has been documented in numerous cocktail manuals dating back to the 19th century. [2] Chicago restaurant critic John Drury included it in his 1931 guide Dining in Chicago , noting that it had been served at the American Bar in Nice and the Embassy Club in London . [3] Whether it originated in Chicago is unknown. [4]

Recipes call for brandy; orange-flavored liqueur , such as curacao or triple sec ; and bitters , stirred or shaken with ice, which may or may not be strained out afterward. [4] [5] In many versions of the drink, it is topped off with champagne [2] [3] [6] [7] or white wine. [4] Some versions call for sugaring the rim of the glass. [4] It can be served on the rocks in a double old-fashioned glass or, especially in the champagne variation, straight up in a champagne coupe or flute or a cocktail glass .
